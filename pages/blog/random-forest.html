Random Forest

Random Forest is an ensemble learning method used for classification, regression, and other tasks, which operates by constructing a multitude of decision trees during training. The output of the random forest is either the mode of the classifications or the mean prediction of the individual trees.

How Random Forest Works

Random Forest works by creating multiple decision trees, each trained on a random subset of the data. These individual trees each make a prediction, and the final output is decided by averaging (for regression) or majority voting (for classification).

  • Step 1: Randomly select subsets from the dataset (with replacement).
  • Step 2: Construct a decision tree for each subset.
  • Step 3: Make predictions with each tree.
  • Step 4: Combine predictions from all trees to make a final prediction.

Advantages of Random Forest

Random Forest is known for its high accuracy and ability to handle large datasets with higher dimensionality. It is also less prone to overfitting compared to individual decision trees.

Conclusion

Random Forest is a powerful and versatile machine learning model that performs well in both classification and regression tasks. Its ensemble nature makes it robust against overfitting and capable of handling complex datasets.
